10-Minute Challenges

Mashup Madness
Task: Describe CIC as a mashup of 2-3 things that are easily recognizable at a BBQ. The goal is to use familiar reference points to quickly convey the essence of CIC. Think about how Kenneth sold “Gold Case” in 30 Rock. How would you sell CIC as the next big thing?
Instructions: 10 mins to think, 3 mins to share, group discussion
Life Stage Metaphor
Task: Compare CIC to another company or celebrity at a particular stage in their journey - the goal is to convey “where we are at right now”. For example, is CIC like Amazon when it was just selling books? Is CIC Apple in Jobs' garage? Are we Simone Biles at the Olympics at the height of her career? How about Bernie Sanders when he was running for president as an underdog?
Instructions: 10 mins to think, 3 mins to share, group discussion
Millionaire Windfall
Task: A wealthy benefactor gives CIC $2 million with no strings attached, but you can’t hire staff. How do you spend the money to maximize impact?
Instructions: 10 mins to think, 3 mins to share, group discussion
Investor Challenge
Task: The wealthy benefactor decides to invest $2m. They expect you to quadruple CIC's revenue within a year. How do you approach this high-stakes challenge?
Instructions: 10 mins to think, 3 mins to share, group discussion
Antimatter
Task: Identify the forces working against CIC. If CIC were an angel, what would be its devil? Describe the opposing forces or challenges.
Instructions: 10 mins to think, 3 mins to share, group discussion
Antidote
Task: There are problems and challenges facing our society. Imagine CIC is the antidote - what is the illness or problem we are solving for?
Instructions: 10 mins to think, 3 mins to share, group discussion
Money Money Money
Task: How does CIC make money? How does CIC make profit? Explain why commercial growth is important.
Instructions: 10 mins to think, 3 mins to share, group discussion
Elevator Pitches - Trading partners
Task: Craft an elevator pitch for trading partners (such as schools, parents, corporates, councils). Tailor the message to their priorities.
Instructions: 10 mins to think, 3 mins to share, group discussion
Elevator Pitches - Development partners
Task: Craft an elevator pitch for development partners (investors, philanthropists, government). Tailor the message to their priorities.
Instructions: 10 mins to think, 3 mins to share, group discussion
Waves of Change
Task: Identify the societal waves CIC is riding, responding to, or pushing against. Describe the larger trends CIC is part of.
Instructions: 10 mins to think, 3 mins to share, group discussion
10-Word Pitch
Task: Summarize CIC in just 10 words. Make it clear, compelling, and concise. Make it clear who your audience is.
Instructions: 10 mins to think, 3 mins to share, group discussion
Learning from the Best
Task: Explore one of the listed websites. What do they do well, and what might CIC learn from how they communicate and describe themselves?
Instructions: 10 mins to think, 3 mins to share, group discussion
  • Reap Benefit: Empowers young people to solve civic and environmental issues in their local communities using technology, data, and citizen action. Visit Reap Benefit
  • Design for Change: A global movement empowering young people to use design-thinking to solve local problems. Visit Design for Change
  • Global Youth Service Day (GYSD): Mobilizes young people to engage in community service projects that address local needs. Visit GYSD
  • DoSomething.org: A global non-profit empowering young people to take action on social issues, from environmental conservation to social justice. Visit DoSomething.org
  • Roots & Shoots: Founded by Dr. Jane Goodall, engaging young people in environmental, conservation, and humanitarian projects. Visit Roots & Shoots
  • WE Schools: Provides educators and students with resources and activities to engage in social justice initiatives. Visit WE Schools
  • Ashoka's Youth Venture: Supports young changemakers in starting their own social ventures with mentoring and resources. Visit WE Schools

5-Minute Challenges

Origin Stories
Task: What was the spark that led you to CIC? Why have you invested your limited time on Earth here?
Instructions: 5 mins to think, 2 mins to share, group discussion
Super Powers
Task: What unique superpower do you bring to the CIC team? Reflect on your strengths and how they contribute to the mission.
Instructions: 5 mins to think, 2 mins to share, group discussion
Napoleon for a Day
Task: You have unilateral power over CIC for one day. What changes would you make, and how would you make your mark?
Instructions: 5 mins to think, 2 mins to share, group discussion
Islands in the Stream
Task: We don’t have to pull alone. Identify potential allies or partners who could help us forward our shared agendas.
Instructions: 5 mins to think, 2 mins to share, group discussion
Wiggles for the Win
Task: Who are we to kids in their journey? Reflect on how CIC fits into their lives.
Instructions: 5 mins to think, 2 mins to share, group discussion
Uber Tales
Task: You’re in an Uber. The driver asks what you do. Simplify or fib—your choice. Describe your work or CIC in a way that makes sense to a curious stranger.
Instructions: 5 mins to think, 2 mins to share, group discussion
Worst Idea Ever
Task: What’s the worst possible next step for CIC? What should we absolutely avoid doing?
Instructions: 5 mins to think, 2 mins to share, group discussion
Hacked and Restricted

Personal Connection

Instructions: 5 mins to think, 2 mins to share, group discussion
Worst Possible Reviews

Instructions: 5 mins to think, 2 mins to share, group discussion
Top Benefits
